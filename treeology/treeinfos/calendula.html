CALENDULA


Calendula (Calendula officinalis) is a plant known as pot marigold.
It is not the same as ornamental marigolds of the Tagetes
genus grown in vegetable gardens. Calendula is native to Asia and southern
Europe and has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and
Unani systems of medicine. The chemicals in calendula might
help new tissue grow in wounds and decrease swelling in the
mouth and throat. Calendula flower is commonly used for wounds, rashes,
infections, inflammation, and many other conditions. But there's
no strong evidence to support the use of calendula for any purpose.
