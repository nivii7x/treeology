FENUGREEK

Fenugreek is a clover-like herb native to the Mediterranean region,
southern Europe, and western Asia. Its seeds, which smell and taste
like maple syrup, have been used in cooking and as medicine.
Fenugreek is used as an ingredient in spice blends and a flavoring
agent in foods, beverages, and tobacco. Fenugreek extracts
are also used in soaps and cosmetics. In North Africa, Asia, and southern
Europe, fenugreek was traditionally used for diabetes
and to increase milk supply in women who were breastfeeding.
